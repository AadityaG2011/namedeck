NameDeck — Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 2026

NameDeck is a flashcard app that helps teachers learn their students' names. It is designed to keep data on your own device. This policy explains what the app does and does not do with information.

What NameDeck stores, and where

  • The student names and photos you add (by typing, importing files, or importing from Google) are stored only on your own device.
  • NameDeck has no server and no account. Your roster and photos are never uploaded to us or to any third party.
  • Uninstalling the app, or using Clear All in My Roster, removes this data from the device.

Google sign-in (optional — only if you use "Import from Google")

  • Signing in is entirely optional and only happens if you choose Import from Google to pull names and photos from your own Google Form responses.
  • NameDeck requests the narrow drive.file permission, which grants access only to the specific spreadsheet and photos you pick — not your wider Google Drive.
  • The app reads the responses you selected and downloads those photos directly to your device. This information is not sent to any NameDeck server (there isn't one).
  • NameDeck does not sell, share, or use your Google data for advertising, and does not use it for any purpose other than importing your roster into the app on your device.

What NameDeck does not do

  • No facial recognition and no biometrics — the app only displays photos.
  • No advertising, and no selling of data.

Children's data

Rosters may contain student information, which teachers manage under their school's authorization. Because that data stays on the teacher's device and is never transmitted to NameDeck, the app does not collect or process it on any server.
